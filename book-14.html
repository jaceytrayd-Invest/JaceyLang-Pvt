The Folio · Book 14

Without the Camp

On the soul that must go forth unto God, apart from what once held His presence

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from Exodus 33 — on Moses, who removed the place of meeting from the midst; on a people who remained where they were; and on the presence of God, no longer dwelling among them, but to be sought outside the camp.

Prologue

The Camp That Remained

The camp remained.
The order held. The people stood as before.
Their tents were set. Their places known.
Nothing outward had been removed.
Yet the Lord — was not in the midst.
O soul — there is a condition
wherein all remains,
yet God is no longer there.
They had not ceased to be a people. They had not cast off the name.
Yet what they had done had altered the place of God among them.
Not destroyed — but displaced.
O soul — sin may not remove thee at once,
yet it may remove God
from where thou standest.
Chapter I

The Tent Removed

Moses took the tent.
Not repaired. Not restored in place.
Removed.
He pitched it afar off.
What had once been central was now without.
O soul — what once stood in the midst
may be set apart,
when God will no longer dwell there.
It was not near. It was not within reach.
It stood — apart.
And this distance was not crossed lightly.
O soul — when God withdraws,
nearness is no longer assumed.
Chapter II

The Going Forth

“Every one which sought the Lord — went out.”
Not all. Not the many. Only those who sought.
They left their place. They passed beyond what was common.
O soul — to seek God,
thou mayest have to depart
from what all others retain.
They rose. Each man at his tent door.
They looked — but did not follow.
They observed the movement — yet remained where they were.
O soul — there are those who will behold thy seeking,
yet will not partake in it.
Chapter III

The Man Who Entered

Moses went in.
Not as before. Not among the people.
Alone.
The cloud descended. The Lord spoke.
But the place of meeting was no longer shared.
O soul — God may meet thee,
yet not restore thee
to what once was.
The Lord came down.
Not to the camp — but to the tent.
His presence remained — yet not where it had been.
O soul — God’s presence may continue,
yet not in the place thou expectest.
They bowed. Each man in his place.
Not at the meeting — but at a distance from it.
Reverence remained — yet separation endured.
O soul — worship may continue,
while distance is not removed.
Chapter IV

The Separation That Endured

The Lord spoke unto Moses.
Face to face — as a man speaketh unto his friend.
The word had not ceased. But it was no longer among all.
O soul — God may still speak,
yet not to the many.
The tent remained without. The camp remained within.
Two places stood — no longer one.
What had been united was now divided.
O soul — there is a dividing God permits,
which no man can reconcile.
No command restored it. No act returned it.
The people remained — and the tent remained apart.
What had been altered was not quickly undone.
O soul — there are losses in God
that are not immediately restored.
Chapter V

The Summons

Wilt thou remain where all still stands?
Wilt thou dwell where God no longer abides?
Or wilt thou go forth — though none go with thee?

O soul — the Lord is to be found,
yet not always where thou hast known Him.
Chapter VI

Without the Camp

W I T H O U T   T H E   C A M P
The camp stood fast in ordered place, Each man within his bound; Yet He who once had filled the midst Was nowhere to be found.
No fire consumed, no voice was heard, Within the common way; Yet still beyond the gathered tents The cloud of glory lay.
Without the camp the meeting stood, Removed from all before; And those who sought the Lord went out, And passed beyond the door.
The many watched, yet did not move, Content where they had been; While one alone drew near to God, And stood where few had seen.
O Lord, if Thou withdraw Thyself,
And leave the place we know;
Give us the will to rise and seek,
And where Thou art, to go.
