

# **Book 05 — Notwithstanding the Lord Stood**

*On divine reckoning amidst abandonment and silence*


**Bible foundation:** Nahum the Elkoshite — a marginal prophet from Samaria, declaring judgment on Nineveh while Judah’s scribes turned aside; God stood with the outcast when men counted him nothing (Nahum 1–3; cf. 2 Kings 17).

---

## **Part 0 — Voice in the Vacuum**

Sixty-five years the land lay dumb.
No oracle rent the heavens, no prophet’s fire burned in the courts.
Samaria’s ruins still smoked in memory; Judah turned its face from the north.
Then came a man from the margins—soil of mingled blood, voice unbidden.
No southern seal rested upon him. No priestly hand upheld him.

O soul, mark this well:
God speaks loudest where silence has reigned longest,
and calls His servants from places men have written off.

---

## **Part 1 — The Outcast’s Commission**

Blood questioned him. Heritage denied him.
Judah’s elders would have barred the gate; Ezra’s line would have turned him away.
Yet the word surged within: *“Arise. Cry against Nineveh.”*
Towers of arrogance rose across the desert, drunk on the blood of nations.
He carried no credentials save the burning coal from heaven’s altar.

O soul, when men deny thy place—thy blood, thy tongue, thy tribe—
remember: divine legitimacy needs no earthly vote.

---

## **Part 2 — Lions and the Teacher Recalled**

Once lions stalked Samaria’s streets because the people knew not the Lord.
A single priest, sent back from exile, taught them fear.
Even foreign kings perceived the necessity of God’s word.
Now another voice from that shadowed soil rose—unwelcomed, yet indispensable.

O soul, thy labor may be invisible to the temple crowd,
but the Almighty stations thee where thy obedience alone restrains judgment.

---

## **Part 3 — Dismissal by the Near**

The scribes of Judah murmured: *“His words touch us not. Let Nineveh tremble; we stand secure in Zion’s shade.”*
They set him aside as one whose hour had passed or never come.
Yet every syllable he uttered was registered where no scroll of men could reach.

O soul, tremble:
The faithful are often scorned by those nearest the sanctuary.
Prophecy in exile’s garment waits for God’s appointed dawn.

---

## **Part 4 — Labor Beneath the Silence**

He walked dust-choked streets. He spoke to hearts of stone.
Injustice rode unchecked; the proud feasted while the oppressed groaned.
No crowd swelled at his feet. No king summoned him to court.
Still his lips moved under heaven’s compulsion, warning of the axe already laid.

O soul, faithfulness in obscurity is not wasted.
It is the hidden forge where God tempers vessels for cosmic reckoning.

---

## **Part 5 — The Tremor of the Ledger**

Men’s ledgers held him at naught—marginal, mixed, uncredentialed.
But heaven’s scales quivered with every uttered warning, every tear for the fallen, every step taken against ridicule.
Nineveh’s doom was decreed; Judah’s comfort secretly prepared.

O soul, do not weigh thy days by human measure.
What passes unseen on earth is monumental before the Judge who forgets nothing.

---

## **Part 6 — Departure Unpraised**

He spoke. He warned. He withdrew into silence once more.
No laurel crowned him. No monument marked his grave.
Yet when Nineveh fell in flood and fire, the word he carried stood vindicated.

O soul, victory often wears the cloak of forgotten labor.
The marginalized are never marginal to God.

---

## **Part 7 — Summons**

Rise, therefore, thou whom the near have cast aside.
Speak where doors are barred. Obey where approval is withheld.
The lions still prowl where knowledge of the Lord grows dim.
Thy voice, though small, may be the very thread by which heaven restrains wrath or looses justice.

For the Lord standeth notwithstanding—
with the outcast, with the silenced, with the one men counted least.

---

## **Part 8 — Poem: Notwithstanding the Lord Stood (Common Meter, 8686)**

From margins where the lions roared
And Samaria lay waste,
A voice arose, by men ignored,
Yet God His seal had placed.

They turned their face, the southern scribes,
And barred the temple gate;
But every word the prophet scribes
Was weighed on scales of fate.

The towers of Nineveh grew tall,
Drunk on the nations’ blood;
He cried their fall though none gave heed,
And heaven’s justice stood.

O soul, endure the silent scorn,
The exile’s thankless part;
The Lord stands fast when all have gone—
He reckons every heart.

When proudest cities crash in flame
And scribes at last confess,
The outcast’s voice shall bear His name
In everlastingness.
