The Folio · Book 16

Seek Them Not

On the soul that has obeyed, endured, and received no rest

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from Jeremiah 45 — on Baruch, who bore the word of another, endured the ruin it proclaimed, and found no rest within; and on the word of the Lord that answered not with relief, but with restraint.

Prologue

The Burden Not His Own

He wrote.
Not his own word — but another’s.
Line upon line, as it was given.
He bore what he did not form. He carried what he could not alter.
O soul — there is a labour in God
wherein thou must bear
what is not thine to shape.
He stood near the prophet. He heard the word before it was spoken aloud.
He was not distant — yet not the source.
The word passed through him — but did not begin in him.
O soul — thou mayest be near the speaking of God,
and yet not stand in its origin.
Chapter I

The Weight and the Ruin

It did not strike at once. It gathered.
Word by word. Line by line.
Until what he wrote began to rest upon him.
O soul — what thou handlest lightly at first
may become weight within thee.
The word did not spare.
It spoke of overthrow. Of breaking. Of a people undone.
He wrote it all. He sealed the judgment with his own hand.
O soul — there is a cost in writing what God has spoken,
when it brings no comfort to man.
He could not stand apart.
The word he wrote became the world he lived in.
The ruin was not elsewhere. It closed around him.
O soul — what God declares may not remain before thee;
it may come upon thee.
Chapter II

The Cry That Broke Through

“Woe is me now.”
Not spoken at the first — but after.
“I fainted in my sighing.”
The strength to carry had given way.
“I find no rest.” Nothing within him answered.
O soul — obedience may continue long
before the soul confesses its weariness.
He did not ask for much.
Not escape. Not reversal.
Only this — that something within would ease. That the burden might relent.
O soul — thou mayest not seek great things,
yet still hope for relief.
Chapter III

The Answer That Withheld

Then came the word.
Not to lift. Not to soothe.
“Behold, that which I have built will I break down…”
The ruin stood. Unchanged.
O soul — God may answer thee,
and leave all things as they are.
Then it came to him:
“Seekest thou great things for thyself?”
Not accusation — but exposure.
What he had not named was named.
O soul — God may reveal in thee
what thou hadst not known thou soughtest.
Chapter IV

The Refusal and the Measure

“Seek them not.”
Not delayed. Not denied later. Refused.
No promise to return. No word of another time.
Only this — cease.
O soul — there are things God will not give thee,
not now, not after.
“But thy life will I give unto thee…”
Not abundance. Not rest.
Life — preserved.
A portion kept — not enlarged.
O soul — God may leave thee with less than thou hadst hoped,
yet not take thee away.
Nothing shifted. The word remained. The ruin advanced. The inward state — unrelieved.
He continued.
O soul — there is a path where obedience remains,
though nothing within thee is restored.
He did not ask again.
Not because he received — but because he understood.
The seeking ceased. Not by satisfaction — but by command.

O soul — there is a ceasing that comes not from fullness, but from restraint.
Chapter V

The Summons

Wilt thou continue when no rest is given?
Wilt thou bear what never becomes light?
Wilt thou obey when nothing returns to thee?

O soul — the Lord may be with thee, and yet give thee no ease.
Chapter VI

Seek Them Not

S E E K   T H E M   N O T
He wrote the word another spoke, And bore what was not his; The lines he traced with careful hand Became his heaviness.
The burden grew with every word, Till strength within him failed; He sought not gain, nor lifted state, Yet still his spirit quailed.
“No rest is found,” his spirit cried, “No ease remains for me”; Yet when the word of God returned, It gave no remedy.
“Seek not for great things,” thus it came, Nor promise yet to be; Only his life was held secure, And that in poverty.
O Lord, when all our strength is spent,
And none relieve our cry;
Give us to cease from seeking more,
And still in Thee abide.
