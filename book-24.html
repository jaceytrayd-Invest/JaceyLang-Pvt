The Folio · Book 24

At the Window

On the soul that remained suspended near the word, until the threshold itself became the place of falling

A prophetic series in ten parts — drawn from Acts 20:7–12 — on the soul that drew near to holy things without ever wholly yielding itself inwardly to them; and on the terrible danger of remaining too long at the threshold between spiritual life and the atmosphere of the natural man.

Prologue

The Place Between

He did not sit outside.
Yet neither did he settle fully within.
He chose the threshold —
the place nearest both worlds.
Behind him: the lamps, the gathered saints, the apostolic word.
Before him: the open night, the moving air, the atmosphere beyond the chamber.
And there he remained —
close enough to hear, yet never wholly enclosed by what he heard.
O soul — what refuses to dwell wholly within the things of God will eventually weaken at the border of them.
Chapter I

The Window

The window promised much.
Nearness without enclosure. Exposure without surrender. Presence without full yielding.
From there he could hear the word while still breathing the air beyond it.
The chamber held the weight of living truth; the window still admitted the softer atmosphere of the natural man.
And because the threshold seemed harmless, he remained there comfortably.
O soul — the soul cannot receive the full burden of spiritual life while still preserving the atmosphere in which the natural man breathes easily.
Chapter II

The Long Speaking

Paul spoke long into the night.
Truth continued steadily through the chamber. Life itself moved through the apostle’s voice.
Yet the danger was no longer distance from the word —
but prolonged nearness without inward surrender.
For the soul may remain beside holy things until their constancy no longer pierces it deeply.
Not because truth has weakened —
but because another atmosphere has slowly begun interpreting it.
O soul — when the natural heart remains unbroken, it will slowly soften the very truth that once sought to pierce it.
Chapter III

The Open Air

The room held light. The window held air.
And somewhere within him, the soul still desired both.
The severity of truth — yet not the full loss of self.
The nearness of life — yet not the inward closing of every other voice.
And so the threshold remained open.
O soul — many desire the living word while still reserving inward openings through which another spirit may continue breathing comfortably.
Chapter IV

The Gradual Sinking

Sleep overcame him.
Not violently. Not suddenly.
The threshold itself made such sleep possible.
For the soul divided long enough eventually grows weary of remaining watchful.
And little by little, the inward man loosened its hold upon attention.
Until hearing became mere exposure, and presence ceased to mean wakefulness.
O soul — the soul rarely notices when holy things have ceased to demand inward participation from it.
Chapter V

The Dangerous Position

Nothing outward appeared alarming.
He still sat near the apostle. Still within reach of the word. Still among the gathered saints.
Yet the danger had entered long before the fall —
in the position itself.
For he had chosen a place where the natural and spiritual continually mingled without resolution.
And eventually, the unresolved threshold weakened him.
O soul — what is never wholly yielded unto God will eventually become unstable at the place where it remains divided.
Chapter VI

The Fall Through the Opening

Then he fell.
Not through rebellion. Not through departure. Not through hatred of truth.
He fell through the very opening he had never fully closed.
The threshold itself became the place of ruin.
And above him still —
the word of life continued sounding through the chamber.
O soul — the openings through which the natural man still breathes freely may become the very places through which the soul eventually falls.
Chapter VII

Beneath the Chamber

He was taken up dead.
And all who remained above suddenly understood the terror of divided habitation.
For the chamber had been real. The lamps had been real. The apostolic word had been real.
Yet none of these prevented the fall of one who never wholly yielded himself inwardly to the room.
O soul — outward nearness to life cannot preserve the soul that inwardly continues dwelling between two atmospheres.
Chapter VIII

The Descent of Mercy

Paul went down.
The word that had filled the upper room now descended beneath it.
Not merely speaking —
stooping.
Embracing the fallen man at the place where dividedness had carried him.
O soul — the mercy of God often descends below the places where hesitation and inward division have cast the soul.
Chapter IX

Life Still in Him

“There is life in him.”
Not because the threshold had preserved him. Not because wakefulness had endured.
But because mercy reached him before the border became final.
And all understood:
the distance between hearing and falling had been far thinner than they imagined.
O soul — many are preserved not because they remained steadfast at the threshold, but because mercy refused to leave them there.
Chapter X

The Summons

Where dost thou dwell inwardly?
Within the chamber — or at the opening?
Hast thou wholly yielded thyself to the word —
or merely remained near enough to hear it?
What windows remain open within thee
through which another atmosphere still enters unjudged?

O soul — the soul cannot remain forever at the threshold; eventually the atmosphere it continues breathing will determine where it falls.
Closing Poem

At the Window

A T   T H E   W I N D O W
He sat beside the living word, Yet near the open air; The chamber held the light of life, The night still breathed him there.
He chose the place ‘twixt room and world, Where both might still be known; Not wholly yielded to the light, Nor wholly darkness-owned.
The apostolic voice went on, The lamps still brightly burned; Yet slowly through dividedness His inward watch was turned.
The window breathed another air That soothed the natural mind; And truth that should have pierced the heart Grew gentler there with time.
And sleep came softly through the breach He never fully sealed; Till life still sounded overhead While death beneath him steal’d.
He fell through that unguarded place Where long he chose to stay; For thresholds weaken souls that try To look in either way.
But mercy stooped below the height Where hesitation fell; And life was found where ruin lay, Though near the edge of hell.
O Lord, shut every inward gate Through which the world still streams; And keep my soul awake within, Not lingering at extremes.
