I Sat Alone

On the soul confined under the hand of God, and the word that wounds its bearer

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from Jeremiah 15 and 20 — on the prophet who bore the word not as power but as burden, and could neither hold it nor be free of it.

Prologue

The Word Received

The word came to him.
Not in comfort. Not in promise.
It entered him. It burned within.
He ate it. And it became his.
At the first, it was as sweetness.
O soul — there is a receiving of the word
that seems light, before its weight is known.
Chapter I

The Separation and the Fire

Then the word set him apart.
He was not sent to gather. He was not given to join.
He stood alone among the people. Yet he could not be as they were. The word within him forbade it.
O soul — what God giveth,
it separateth before it sustaineth.
“Take thee not a wife.”
No house was to be built. No sons to be raised. No future was permitted him among the living.
The word did not only speak — it ordered his life against itself.
O soul — obedience may close
the very paths thy heart would have chosen.
“I sat not in the assembly.”
He did not rejoice with them. He did not join their feasting.
The hand of the Lord was upon him. He sat alone. The word had taken him out from among men.
O soul — there is a loneliness
that is not loss, but appointment.
He would have held it back. He would have remained silent.
But the word burned within him. It was as fire shut up in his bones. He could not stay it.
O soul — the word of God doth not remain
where it is resisted;
it presses until it is spoken.
Chapter II

The Reproach and the Weariness

Then came the reproach.
“Magor-missabib.” “Terror on every side.”
They mocked him. They watched for his halting.
The word he bore became his shame before men.
O soul — what God speaketh through thee
may turn men against thee.
He grew weary.
Not in the speaking — but in the bearing.
“Why is my pain perpetual?”
The wound did not close. The burden did not lift. The word remained.
O soul — there is a weariness
that comes not from labour,
but from enduring.
Chapter III

The Accusation and the Rebuke

“Wilt Thou be unto me as a liar?”
The cry broke forth. Not hidden. Not restrained.
He spoke as one wounded by the very word he carried. The complaint rose against God Himself.
O soul — there are depths
where even faith speaks in anguish
against the Lord.
“If thou return, then will I bring thee again.”
The Lord answered.
Not by removing the burden. But by calling him back under it.
“Take forth the precious from the vile.”
Let them return unto thee — but return not thou unto them.
O soul — God doth not yield to the wounded cry;
He restores the man to the word.
“I will make thee unto this people a fenced brasen wall.”
Not release — but endurance.
They would fight against him. They would not prevail. The word remained his portion.
O soul — strength is given
not to escape the burden,
but to remain under it.
Chapter IV

The Day Cursed

Yet the wound spoke again.
“Cursed be the day wherein I was born.”
The lament broke deeper.
He did not curse the Lord — but he cursed his own beginning. The burden had entered his very life.
O soul — there is a suffering
that reaches back to the root of thy being.
“Wherefore came I forth out of the womb?”
To see labour. To see sorrow. That his days should be consumed with shame.
No answer followed. No ease was given. The question remained.
O soul — not every cry is answered;
some are carried.
Chapter V

The Summons

Wilt thou bear the word when it isolates thee?
Wilt thou speak when it brings thee reproach?
Wilt thou remain when it wounds thee?

For the servant is not above the word he carries.

And the word of the Lord is not given for thy ease —
but for His purpose.
Chapter VI

I Sat Alone

I   S A T   A L O N E
The word I took within my soul Was sweet upon the tongue; Yet soon it burned within my bones, Too strong to be undone.
It drove me from the place of men, It left me standing lone; The laughter ceased, the voice grew dim — The burden was my own.
I cried to God in weariness, And questioned what He gave; Yet still the fire remained in me, And would not let me rest.
O soul, if thou must bear His word,
Then bear it to the end;
For God doth keep the one He wounds,
And stands where none defend.
