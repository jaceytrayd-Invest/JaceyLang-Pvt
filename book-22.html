The Folio · Book 22

Not Found in Thy Book

On the soul that continues in the way, yet begins to fear it may not be numbered among those God has known

A prophetic series in ten parts — drawn from Exodus 32 and Malachi 3 — on the soul that has continued without witness, and now begins to fear that its continuance may not be held before God at all; and on the unseen record that is not opened to the one who lives beneath it.

“Whosoever hath sinned against Me, him will I blot out of My book.”  ·  “And a book of remembrance was written before Him…”

Prologue

The Thought That Was Not There Before

It did not begin early.
It came after.
After continuance. After endurance. After remaining.
Then — this entered:
What if I am not among them?
Thou didst not come to Me with this at the beginning.

I allowed thee to continue first.
I allowed thee to endure.
I allowed the years to pass —
and then, when the noise of early faith had quieted,
I brought this near.

Not to destroy thee —
but because thou wert now able to bear it.
O soul — there is a fear that does not arise at the beginning, but after long standing. And it is I who permitted it to enter.
Chapter I

The Book Not Seen

It is written.
But not shown.
Spoken of — but not opened.
Its contents remain beyond thee.
I have not shown thee the book.

Not because thou art excluded —
but because the keeping of it belongs to Me alone.

What I record, I do not place within thy keeping.
What I remember, I do not require thee to confirm.

The book is before Me.
That is enough — whether thou canst see it or not.
O soul — what God records, He does not place within thy keeping. The record is His. Thy part is only to continue.
Chapter II

The Loss of Assumed Place

Once, it was not questioned.
To continue was to belong.
But now —
continuance no longer assures it.
I have taken from thee the easy certainty.

The confidence that said: I am among them, for I have remained.
Hear Me now — and hear Me alone, for this is not for others to receive:

Remaining is not the same as being known.
Continuance is not the same as inscription.

I stripped the assumption not to cast thee out,
but to teach thee that thy standing rests in Me —
not in thy duration.
O soul — there is a point where even faithfulness ceases to confirm thy standing. I am the confirmer. Not thy record of continuance.
Chapter III

The Separation Becomes Personal

It was once general:
the righteous and the wicked.
Now it narrows:
those recorded — and those not.
And thou art within neither sight.
The line thou once drew between thyself and others
I have now drawn through thee.

This is what thou canst not speak to those around thee.
They would not understand it.
They would offer comfort —
and comfort is not what I am doing here.

I am drawing thee near enough to the question
that thou canst no longer stand outside it
and speak of it as another man’s trouble.
O soul — God’s distinctions may draw near enough to include thee in their territory, yet withhold from thee the knowledge of where thou standest within them.
Chapter IV

The Inability to Read Oneself

No sign declares it.
No inward mark confirms it.
Nothing within thee answers the question.
Thou hast searched thyself for the evidence.

Thou hast looked for the mark that says: thou art Mine.

I did not place that mark where thou canst read it.
O soul — thou canst not read thyself into the book of God. That is not thy task. Continue. I am the reader.
The evidence is with Me.
The witness is before Me.
The seal is hidden — not because I am cruel,
but because if thou couldst read it,
thou wouldst trust the reading
and cease to need Me.
Chapter V

The Weight of Possibility

Not certainty — but possibility.
Not conclusion — but inability to dismiss it.
That thou mayest not be known as thou hadst believed.
I have not told thee that thou art excluded.

Hear this clearly, and do not share it,
for it is between thee and Me alone.
I have also not removed the weight of not knowing.

This is the burden I have permitted thee to carry —
the burden of perhaps.

It is heavier than loss.
Loss has a name. This does not.
O soul — there is a burden heavier than loss: the thought that thou mayest not belong at all. I know thou bearest it. I placed it near thee. Continue.
And I am the only one who can lift it —
in My time, not thine.
Chapter VI

The Continuance Under Uncertainty

He did not stop.
He did not withdraw.
But now he continued without knowing if his continuance was held.
This is what I ask of thee now.

Not to feel certain.
Not to manufacture assurance.
Not to find a word from another
that quiets what only I can quiet.
O soul — there is a remaining that persists without any assurance that it is received. That remaining, when it is genuine, is itself a witness — though thou canst not see it.
I ask thee to continue —
without the confirmation thou desirest.
This is not cruelty.
This is the deepest form of trust I can ask from thee:
to walk with Me when thou canst not tell
whether I have written thy name
or left that page unmarked.
Chapter VII

The Silence of Heaven Toward the Question

No answer came.
Not to confirm. Not to deny.
The question stood — unresolved.
I heard it.

Every time thou didst bring it before Me in the night —
when no other soul was near,
when the words could not be formed to say to another,
when even thy own understanding of the question was unclear —
I heard it.
And I chose not to answer it.
My silence is not My absence.
My silence, in this matter, is My dealings with thee.
The answer, when I give it, will not come
through the means thou hast been watching.
O soul — God may leave thee beneath a question He does not answer. His silence toward it is not His indifference to thee. He heard. He is choosing the hour of reply.
Chapter VIII

The Stripping of Claim

He could no longer say:
I am among them.
Not because he knew he was not — but because he could no longer say that he was.
I took the words from thee.

Not the belonging — but the claim to belonging.
There is a man who says “I am His”
because he has always said it.
There is another who cannot say it anymore —
not from unbelief,
but because I have made the claim too weighty
for his own mouth to carry.
O soul — there is a removing of claim, even without a removing of place. The man who can no longer say “I am His” with ease may be the one whose name is most carefully held.
That second man is nearer to Me than the first imagines.

Thou art the second man.
Chapter IX

The Place Without Name

He stood.
Still within the way. Still under the word.
Yet without name — without certainty.
Thou art not outside My presence.

Thou art standing in a place
that has no name in the language
those around thee use for such things.
They speak of assurance.
They speak of peace.
They speak of the witness of the Spirit.

And thou art silent —
because what thou hast is none of those things,
and yet thou hast not left.
O soul — thou mayest remain where God is, and yet not know thyself as belonging there. The place has a name before Him, even when it has none before thee.
I know the name of this place.
It is enough that I know it.
Chapter X

The Summons

Wilt thou continue without knowing thy place?
Wilt thou remain without assurance of being known?
Wilt thou walk when thou canst not tell if thy name is before Him?

O soul — the Lord may call thee to endure even the loss of knowing whether thou art His.
Then hear Me now — and let no other hear this word, for it is spoken to thee alone, in a register the room around thee cannot perceive:

I am asking thee to continue.

Not with certainty.
Not with confirmed belonging.
Not with the knowledge of thy name in My book.

But with this one thing:
the willingness to keep walking toward Me
even when thou canst not tell
whether I have marked the page or left it bare.

That willingness — I have seen it in thee.
And I do not overlook what I have seen.

Continue, soul.
Continue.
Closing Poem

Not Found in Thy Book

N O T   F O U N D   I N   T H Y   B O O K
A book is writ before the Lord, Its pages none may see; And those inscribed are known of Him, Yet hidden utterly.
Once I had thought my place was sure, Nor questioned where I stood; But long continuance stripped the claim That early standing would.
I walk the path I walked before, Yet cannot say I’m known; No voice confirms, no mark appears To claim me as Thine own.
The question stands unanswered still, No heaven-word has come; And yet I have not turned from Thee, Though I am stricken dumb.
Those near me speak of peace they hold, Of witness inly known; I cannot speak their language now — I stand in this alone.
Yet something will not let me leave, Though nothing bids me stay; A gravity not named by me Has held me to this way.
O Lord, if I am held by Thee, It lies beyond my sight; Keep me beneath Thy hidden will, And lead me through the night.
And if Thou hast inscribed my name Where I shall never see — Then let that knowledge rest with Thee, And let my rest be Thee.
