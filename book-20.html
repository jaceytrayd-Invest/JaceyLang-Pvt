After This No More Asking

On the soul where the need to understand has ceased

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from John 16:23 — on the soul that has asked, wrestled, obeyed, and yielded; and now enters a state where questions no longer rise — not from fullness of knowledge, but from the passing away of the need to know.

Prologue

The Question That Did Not Rise

It did not come.
Not suppressed. Not resisted.
Simply — absent.
What once formed quickly — now did not form at all.
O soul — there is a state
where the question itself does not return.
Chapter I

The Memory of Asking

It had been constant.
Why this? Why now? Why thus?
The soul had lived in inquiry.
Now —
it remembered asking, but did not ask.
O soul — what once defined thee
may pass without announcement.
Chapter II

The Absence and the End of Interpreting

It was not void.
Not dull. Not indifferent.
Something remained —
but without movement toward explanation.
O soul — there is a quiet
that is not emptiness,
but the stilling of demand.
Events continued. Nothing ceased.
Yet nothing was pursued for meaning.
What came — was left as it came.
O soul — there is a place
where thou no longer seekest
to interpret what God allows.
Chapter III

The Release of Why

Not answered — but gone. Not resolved — but unnecessary.
The question had not been satisfied.
It had been outlived.
O soul — some questions do not receive answers;
they lose their hold.
He walked.
As before — yet not as before.
Steps remained — but without inward reaching.
O soul — there is a walking
that does not look ahead
to understand its path.
Chapter IV

The Peace and the Relation That Remained

It was not declared. Not felt as relief.
Yet it did not press.
Nothing within insisted on knowing.
O soul — peace may come,
not as answer,
but as the absence of demand.
God had not changed. The way had not altered.
Only this —
the need to grasp Him had fallen away.
O soul — there is a knowing of God
that does not arise from understanding Him.
No moment marked it.
No line was crossed.
It simply was — and continued.

O soul — the deepest changes may come without recognition.
Chapter V

The Summons

Wilt thou remain when thou no longer askest?
Wilt thou walk when nothing within seeks to know?
Wilt thou abide when understanding no longer rises?

O soul — the Lord may bring thee beyond asking,
yet not beyond following.
Chapter VI

After This No More Asking

A F T E R   T H I S   N O   M O R E   A S K I N G
The questions once would rise in haste, And press me through the night; I sought to read Thy hidden ways, And bring Thy dealings right.
No rest was found in what I learned, Nor peace in what I proved; For still the weight of things unseen Within my spirit moved.
But now no such arising comes, Though all is yet unclear; The voice that once demanded cause No longer labours here.
No answer came to still the mind, No light to make it plain; Yet something deeper than reply Has loosed me from the strain.
I do not search as once I did, Nor strive to understand; Yet still I walk the path Thou set’st, And yield me to Thy hand.
The need to know has passed away, Though nothing has been shown; And I remain before Thy will, Content to be Thine own.
O Lord, if silence be Thy way,
And questions cease in me;
Then keep me in that quiet place,
Still bound to follow Thee.
