The Folio · Book 13

By Whom the Word Came

On the soul that can no longer discern, and must receive without sight

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from 2 Kings 22 and 2 Chronicles 34 — on Josiah who heard the lost Book and could not discern it; on Hilkiah who brought it forth; and on Huldah the prophetess, by whom the word of the Lord was confirmed when all inward witness had failed.

Prologue

The Beginning That Promised Well

A king arose — yet a child.
The years had not formed him, yet his heart inclined aright.
He turned early. He sought what he did not yet know.
The land bore its ruin openly — altars misset, ways long corrupted.
Yet under his hand, a turning began. What was broken was no longer left untouched.
And hope moved — quiet, but real.
O soul — there are beginnings that appear right before God,
yet leave thee still unable to discern Him.
Chapter I

The Work Before the Word

He did what he could.
He removed. He cast down. He set in order what lay within reach.
The land shifted outwardly — yet the ground within remained uncertain.
For he acted without a word fully known.
O soul — zeal may begin the work,
yet cannot supply the light.
Then it was found.
Not given anew — but uncovered.
It had lain while they laboured. It had spoken — while they did not hear.
And now it stood before them, not as guide — but as witness.
O soul — what thou lackest
may already be present,
yet unknown to thee.
Chapter II

The Book Found and the Hearing

It was read.
No voice added. No meaning softened.
Only the word — as it stood.
And in the hearing, his strength gave way.
Not because it was unclear — but because it was too clear.
O soul — truth may not guide thee first;
it may undo thee.
He rent his garments.
Not in custom — but in certainty lost.
What he had begun stood questioned. What he had trusted stood exposed.
He could not return to what he had been.
O soul — there is a knowing
that strips thee of all former ground.
Chapter III

The Question That Could Not Be Answered Within

“Go — inquire.”
Not for meaning — but for truth.
The word had been heard, yet not discerned.
Was this the voice of God — or the weight of a text too long ignored?
He could not tell.
O soul — there is a depth
where thou canst no longer distinguish
between what is divine and what is dreadful.
No witness rose. No inward light confirmed.
The heart remained — unsettled. The word stood — yet not within him.
O soul — God may speak,
and yet leave thee without
the sense of His speaking.
So they turned.
Not to interpret — but to know. Not in assurance — but in lack.
If truth were to be known, it must come from beyond them.
O soul — when thou canst not discern,
thou art driven outside thyself.
Chapter IV

The Voice Not Foreseen

They came where the word was.
Not to one prepared in their thought — but to one appointed.
She did not extend her voice. She did not seek their need.
She answered.
O soul — God is not bound to thy expectation
in declaring His word.
“Thus saith the Lord.”
It came — without opening.
The judgment stood. The course was fixed. Nothing was lessened. Nothing was delayed.
What had been feared was confirmed.
O soul — there are words from God
that remove all hope of alteration.
Chapter V

The Word and What Remained

No path was shown. No future made clear.
Only this remained: it would be so.
He knew — yet did not see.
O soul — certainty may be given thee,
yet without any light to walk by.
He could not return to himself.
Not to his judgment. Not to his sense. Not to his beginning.
All had proved insufficient.
He stood — dependent on what had come to him.
O soul — there is a breaking
wherein thou canst no longer rely upon thyself
to know God.
Yet he moved.
Not from clarity. Not from understanding.
But from the word that could not be set aside.
He did not see — yet he did not refuse.
O soul — obedience may remain
when all sight is taken.
The judgment would come. The word would stand. Nothing had been turned.

Yet this remained:
He no longer trusted what arose within,
but what had been declared.

O soul — when sight is not given, thou art left with this:
to receive what stands, though thou canst not see.
Chapter VI

When Sight Has Failed

W H E N   S I G H T   H A S   F A I L E D
Thy holy Book before them lay, Yet none discerned it right; For long they walked in borrowed form, And lost the inward light.
The words were heard, yet left them torn, No witness rose within; They feared to trust what seemed from Thee, Yet dared not call it sin.
They stood between two silent depths, Not knowing where to stand; If this were voice, or only dread, They could not understand.
Then by a hand they had not sought, Thy word to them was sent; Not proved by sense, nor sealed within, Yet held with full consent.
No light was given for the way, No promise turned aside; Only a word that must be borne, With all that it implied.
O Lord, when all within is dark,
And sight and sense have fled;
Establish Thou Thy word alone,
And bow the heart to dread.
