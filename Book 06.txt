
# **Book 06 — Before the Lord He Stood**

*On defilement, accusation, and the imputed restoration of God*
**Biblical foundation:** Joshua the High Priest before the Angel of the Lord; Zechariah 3; Zerubbabel and the temple not by might but by Spirit (Zechariah 4); echoed in Haggai 2.

---

## **Part 0 — The Sight That Could Not Be Hidden**

He stood.

Not in the court of men—
but before the Lord.

Garments stained.
Defilement undisguised.
Office yet intact—
yet the stain undeniable.

No argument formed.
No defence remained.

O soul—
there is a standing before God
where every covering fails.

---

## **Part 1 — The Accuser at His Right Hand**

And there—
at his right hand—
stood the accuser.

Not with tumult.
Not with striving.
Only with certainty.

This stain.
This fault.
This unfitness.

And heaven did not gainsay it.

O soul—
there are accusations
that are true.

---

## **Part 2 — Silence of the Priest**

He answered nothing.

No plea.
No explanation.
No recollection of former service.

Years at the altar—
silent.

Sacrifice upon sacrifice—
silent.

The garment spoke
beyond all memory.

O soul—
there comes a moment
when thy record cannot speak for thee.

---

## **Part 3 — The Rebuke from Above**

“The Lord rebuke thee.”

Not Joshua.
Not priesthood.
Not merit.

The Lord Himself.

“Is not this a brand
plucked from the fire?”

Not clean—
yet chosen.

Not worthy—
yet spared.

O soul—
thy standing is not defended—
it is declared.

---

## **Part 4 — Removal Without Request**

“Take away the filthy garments.”

He did not ask.
He did not frame repentance in that hour.
He did not strive for cleansing.

He stood.

And God spoke.

And it was done.

O soul—
there is a cleansing
thou dost not initiate.

---

## **Part 5 — Change of Raiment**

“Behold—
I have caused thine iniquity
to pass from thee.”

Not veiled.
Not deferred.

Caused to pass.

“And I will clothe thee.”

Garments new.
Clean.
Bestowed—
not earned.

O soul—
God does not repair the old garment.
He removes it.

---

## **Part 6 — The Fair Mitre Set**

“Let them set a fair mitre upon his head.”

Authority restored.
Office reappointed.

Not by process—
but by decree.

Not by proving—
but by will.

O soul—
what God restores,
He restores without counsel.

---

## **Part 7 — Condition After Grace**

“If thou wilt walk in My ways…”

Now comes the charge.

Not before cleansing—
after.

“Then thou shalt judge My house.”
“Then thou shalt keep My courts.”

Responsibility follows mercy.

O soul—
grace is not the conclusion—
it is the beginning of weight.

---

## **Part 8 — The Stone Set Before Him**

A stone was laid.

Seven eyes upon it.

Its engraving—
not of man.

“I will remove the iniquity of that land
in one day.”

Not by slow remedy—
but by act.

O soul—
God completes in a moment
what years cannot perfect.

---

## **Part 9 — Zerubbabel’s Weak Hands**

Beside him—
another stood.

Zerubbabel.

No throne.
No host.
No sufficiency for the work.

The foundation laid—
yet the labour resisted.

O soul—
vision often exceeds strength.

---

## **Part 10 — Not by Might**

“Not by might.
Nor by power.
But by My Spirit.”

The mountain—
not ascended.

Removed.

The temple—
not forced into being.

Raised
by breath unseen.

O soul—
thy insufficiency
is no impediment to God.

---

## **Part 11 — The Day of Small Things**

“Who hath despised
the day of small things?”

A beginning slighted.
A work unfinished.
Hands uncertain in their labour.

Yet heaven rejoiced
to behold the plummet.

O soul—
what seems small
is not small above.

---

## **Part 12 — Filth Remembered, Yet Gone**

The garments were gone—
yet the memory endured.

He knew
what he had been.

Yet he stood—
restored.

O soul—
God removes iniquity,
yet leaves remembrance
to humble the walk.

---

## **Part 13 — The Temple That Could Not Rise**

Stone upon stone—
yet resistance.

Voice against voice.
Hand against hand.

The work delayed.
The vision hindered.

O soul—
even that which is of God
meets with opposition.

---

## **Part 14 — The Lord Who Finishes**

“The hands of Zerubbabel
have laid the foundation—
his hands shall also finish it.”

Not by his sufficiency—
but by God’s word.

Completion
is bound to promise.

O soul—
what God begins through thee,
He Himself undertakes to finish.

---

## **Part 15 — Summons**

Come, stand.

Not cleansed—
but willing.

Not worthy—
but called.

Let the accusation speak.
Let the garment be seen.

And remain—
until the Lord answers.

O soul—
thy restoration is not negotiated.
It is declared.

---

## **Part 16 — Poem: Garments Exchanged**

Before the throne the priest was found,
His garments stained with sin;
No plea arose, no righteous sound,
No strength was left within.

The accuser stood at his side,
Each fault laid bare to view;
Yet God Himself the charge denied,
And spoke what none else knew.

“Remove the filth, and clothe him fair,”
The word of mercy came;
Not by his worth, nor by his care,
But by the Lord’s own name.

O soul, stand still before thy God,
Let every covering fall;
For He who marks the guilty rod
Can cleanse and crown it all.
