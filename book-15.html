The Folio · Book 15

Not in the Fire

On the soul that has gone forth unto God, yet cannot find Him in what appears

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from 1 Kings 19 — on Elijah, who returned to the mount of former revealing, and found no certainty in the signs that once declared God; and on the voice that followed, which did not restore what had been lost.

Prologue

The Place That Should Have Answered

He came.
The road had held. The distance had not deceived him.
He stood where God had once made Himself known.
Nothing in the journey had failed.
Yet the place itself gave back nothing.
O soul — thou mayest arrive at the ground of revelation,
and find it does not answer thee.
This was not unknown. It had once borne fire. It had once carried certainty.
He stood within what should have remembered.
The past remained true — yet it did not speak.
O soul — what God has done may abide in truth,
yet cease to sustain thee.
He did not ask for another God.
He waited for the One he knew.
Quietly. Completely.
Yet his waiting was already shaped.

O soul — thou mayest seek the living God,
and yet expect Him only as He has been.
Chapter I

The Signs That Failed

It came.
The wind — strong enough to rend the rock.
Nothing withstood it. It bore the likeness of power.
Yet something within him did not bow.
O soul — there are workings that resemble God,
yet carry not His presence.
Then the ground gave way.
What had stood was made uncertain. The earth itself failed beneath him.
Yet still — nothing spoke.
O soul — what shakes thee
may not be God who moves.
Then came the fire.
As once before. As memory held it.
It rose — familiar, unquestioned.
Yet now — it passed without Him.
O soul — God is not bound to dwell
in what He has once used.
Chapter II

The Collapse of Recognition

Nothing remained.
Not the wind. Not the shaking. Not the fire.
Each had come with force — and left him empty.
He could not find God in anything that appeared.
O soul — there is an hour when every sign fails,
and all former knowing is taken from thee.
Chapter III

What Remained

After.
When all had spent itself — something remained.
Not stronger. Not clearer.
Quieter than speech.
It did not declare itself. It did not compel.
Yet it could not be set aside.
O soul — what is of God may come without form,
yet remain beyond refusal.
He heard. But he could not retain it.
It gave him nothing to hold — no image, no method, no return.
It simply was. And that was enough.
O soul — God may speak,
and leave thee with nothing thou canst secure.
He covered his face.
Not in knowledge — but in yielding.
He did not understand — yet he bowed.
O soul — submission may arise
where understanding is denied.
Chapter IV

The Question and the Sending

“What doest thou here?”
It remained. Not answered. Not altered.
Nothing within him had been resolved.
O soul — God may meet thee,
and yet leave thee
within the same question.
He was sent.
Not restored. Not relieved.
Nothing was made easier. Only the way ahead — unsoftened.
O soul — God may speak,
and yet not lessen what thou must bear.
He departed. He could not say what had passed.
He carried nothing he could name. The place remained — unchanged.
O soul — thou mayest leave the presence of God
without possessing what was given there.
Chapter V

The Summons

Wilt thou remain when nothing reveals Him?
Wilt thou bow when thou canst not know?
Wilt thou go on when nothing is secured?

O soul — God is not known by what appears,
but by what cannot be refused.
Chapter VI

Not in the Fire

N O T   I N   T H E   F I R E
He came unto the mount of God, Where once the fire had shone; Yet every sign that met his soul Left him to stand alone.
The wind arose and brake the rock Before his watching eye; The Lord was not within the wind, Though power was passing by.
The earth beneath him shook and failed, As though all ground would end; The Lord was not within the quake, Nor in its fearful rend.
The fire returned as once before, With form he knew full well; The Lord was not within the flame, Though once He there did dwell.
Then after all had spent itself, A voice without a frame; No sign was left for him to hold, Yet he could not disclaim.
O Lord, when all we know of Thee
Shall fail before our sight;
Give us to bow beneath Thy word,
And walk without Thy light.
