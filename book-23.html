A House Too Noisy for Wisdom

On the soul that slowly loses the inward quietness where the voice of God is heard

A prophetic series in twelve parts — drawn from Proverbs 1–9, 14, 21, 25, and 27 — on the soul that gradually became at home with inward noise, hidden contention, and permitted disturbance, until wisdom no longer rested within its chambers as before.

Prologue

When Quietness Began to Leave the House

Nothing scandalous had entered.
The prayers remained. The Scriptures remained. The outward order remained.
Yet inwardly —
quietness had begun to depart.
Small irritations lingered longer. Words became sharper. Thoughts became noisier. Hidden striving filled the chambers.
And though holy things still stood within the house —
wisdom no longer seemed to rest there as before.
O soul — what continually disturbs thy inward quietness will eventually alter what voices can still be heard there.
Chapter I

The Woman Wisdom Built

Wisdom did not build quickly.
Her house rose slowly —
through restraint, through correction, through fear of the Lord.
Her chambers were not loud. They were clean.
Nothing clamoured there. Nothing seduced there. Nothing hurried the soul away from reverence.
O soul — wisdom does not merely instruct the heart; she preserves the inward conditions by which God may still be heard.
Chapter II

The Strange Woman

She did not first oppose truth.
She softened vigilance.
Her words were smooth. Her spirit relieving.
Near her, the soul no longer felt the strain of watchfulness.
And because nothing seemed openly broken —
the danger appeared small.
O soul — folly rarely asks thee first to forsake God; she asks only that thou cease guarding what keeps thee near Him.
Chapter III

The Contentious Atmosphere

The house became heavy.
Not through catastrophe —
through continual friction.
Sharp words remained in the air. Hidden irritations multiplied. Quietness could no longer settle there.
Even silence felt restless.
O soul — there are inward climates where wisdom still speaks, yet nothing within remains still enough to receive her.
Chapter IV

The Dripping Roof

It was not one wound. Not one rebellion.
But continual disturbance.
Small resentments. Repeated vanity. Unrestrained speech. Constant inward agitation.
Like water falling endlessly in the night —
the soul slowly wearied beneath it.
O soul — many lose the fear of God not through open wickedness, but through the slow exhaustion of continual inward unrest.
Chapter V

The Hidden Chambers

Nothing outward yet appeared ruined.
But hidden rooms had formed within.
Imaginations visited them. Secret indulgences furnished them. Unspoken desires rested there comfortably.
And the soul discovered —
what is entertained inwardly eventually alters the atmosphere of the whole house.
O soul — whatever thou repeatedly givest shelter to within thee will quietly begin governing what can dwell there beside it.
Chapter VI

The Father's Cry

“My son…”
Again and again the warning came.
Not coldly. Not harshly.
Urgently.
As though wisdom foresaw a loss deeper than outward failure.
Not merely sin —
but the gradual inability to hear rightly anymore.
O soul — the warnings of God are often less about preventing thy fall than preserving thy capacity to hear Him before thy inward ear grows dull.
Chapter VII

The House Folly Loved

Folly did not fear noise.
She multiplied it.
Pleasure without restraint. Words without meekness. Desire without governing. Religion without trembling.
And because the flesh found ease there —
the soul remained.
O soul — whatever continually quiets conviction will eventually become more welcome to thee than truth itself.
Chapter VIII

Better the Corner of the Roof

Better solitude. Better wilderness. Better dry bread in peace.
Why?
Because wisdom can still breathe there.
The soul can still hear there.
O soul — peace is not merely comfort to the righteous; it is one of the chambers in which the fear of the Lord survives.
Chapter IX

When Wisdom Grew Distant

She had not ceased calling.
But the house had grown too full.
Too restless. Too crowded. Too inwardly loud.
And though truth was still acknowledged —
its voice no longer carried deeply into the chambers.
O soul — it is possible to honour wisdom publicly while inwardly living in conditions that make her presence unwelcome.
Chapter X

The Discovery

Then at last —
the soul perceived it.
The issue had not only been certain sins.
It had been atmosphere.
A whole inward climate had formed —
where holiness felt strained, silence felt uncomfortable, and prayer no longer rested naturally within the walls.
O soul — the spirit ruling thy inward house will determine whether the voice of God remains a guest there or a stranger.
Chapter XI

The Mercy of Seeing

Yet this remained:
the house was seen.
Not restored instantly. Not purified at once.
But exposed.
And exposure itself became mercy.
For wisdom still cried aloud outside the chambers.
O soul — there is mercy wherever God still permits thee to see clearly what spirit has been dwelling within thee.
Chapter XII

The Summons

What atmosphere fills thy inward house?
What spirit rests easily there?
What no longer troubles thee that once would have driven thee to prayer?

O soul — every soul becomes, in time, a habitation suited to the voices it continually welcomes.
Closing Poem

A House Too Noisy for Wisdom

A   H O U S E   T O O   N O I S Y   F O R   W I S D O M
The house still kept its outward form, Its prayers and sacred word; Yet inward noise had slowly grown, Till wisdom scarce was heard.
No open darkness entered there, No sudden fall from grace; Yet sharper thoughts and restless speech Had filled the quiet place.
The dripping of continual strife Wore softly through the night; And hidden chambers welcomed things That fled the holy light.
The strange woman did not compel, She only calmed the fear; And made the watch of holiness Seem hard to persevere.
But wisdom builds with reverent hands, And dwells where peace remains; She does not shout above the flesh, Nor rule through inward strains.
O Lord, search all the rooms within Where noisier spirits move; And make my inward house again A place for holy love.
