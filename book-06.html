Before the Lord He Stood

On defilement, accusation, and the imputed restoration of God

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from Zechariah 3–4 and Haggai 2 — on Joshua the High Priest before the Angel of the Lord; on Zerubbabel and the temple raised not by might but by Spirit.

Prologue

The Sight That Could Not Be Hidden

He stood.
Not in the court of men — but before the Lord.
Garments stained. Defilement undisguised. Office yet intact — yet the stain undeniable.
No argument formed. No defence remained.
O soul —
there is a standing before God
where every covering fails.
Chapter I

Accusation and Silence

And there — at his right hand — stood the accuser.
Not with tumult. Not with striving. Only with certainty.
This stain. This fault. This unfitness.
And heaven did not gainsay it.
O soul —
there are accusations
that are true.
He answered nothing.
No plea. No explanation. No recollection of former service.
Years at the altar — silent. Sacrifice upon sacrifice — silent.
The garment spoke beyond all memory.
O soul —
there comes a moment
when thy record cannot speak for thee.
Chapter II

The Rebuke and the Removal

“The Lord rebuke thee.”
Not Joshua. Not priesthood. Not merit.
The Lord Himself.
“Is not this a brand plucked from the fire?”
Not clean — yet chosen. Not worthy — yet spared.
O soul —
thy standing is not defended —
it is declared.
“Take away the filthy garments.”
He did not ask. He did not frame repentance in that hour. He did not strive for cleansing.
He stood.
And God spoke. And it was done.
O soul —
there is a cleansing
thou dost not initiate.
Chapter III

Restoration and Its Weight

“Behold — I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee.”
Not veiled. Not deferred.
Caused to pass.
“And I will clothe thee.”
Garments new. Clean. Bestowed — not earned.
O soul —
God does not repair the old garment.
He removes it.
“Let them set a fair mitre upon his head.”
Authority restored. Office reappointed.
Not by process — but by decree. Not by proving — but by will.
O soul —
what God restores,
He restores without counsel.
“If thou wilt walk in My ways…”
Now comes the charge.
Not before cleansing — after.
“Then thou shalt judge My house.” “Then thou shalt keep My courts.”
Responsibility follows mercy.
O soul —
grace is not the conclusion —
it is the beginning of weight.
A stone was laid. Seven eyes upon it. Its engraving — not of man.
“I will remove the iniquity of that land in one day.”
Not by slow remedy — but by act.
O soul —
God completes in a moment
what years cannot perfect.
Chapter IV

Not by Might

Beside him — another stood.
Zerubbabel.
No throne. No host. No sufficiency for the work.
The foundation laid — yet the labour resisted.
O soul —
vision often exceeds strength.
“Not by might.
Nor by power.
But by My Spirit.”

The mountain — not ascended. Removed.
The temple — not forced into being.
Raised by breath unseen.
O soul —
thy insufficiency
is no impediment to God.
“Who hath despised the day of small things?”
A beginning slighted. A work unfinished. Hands uncertain in their labour.
Yet heaven rejoiced to behold the plummet.
O soul —
what seems small
is not small above.
The garments were gone — yet the memory endured.
He knew what he had been.
Yet he stood — restored.
O soul —
God removes iniquity,
yet leaves remembrance
to humble the walk.
Stone upon stone — yet resistance. Voice against voice. Hand against hand. The work delayed. The vision hindered.
O soul —
even that which is of God
meets with opposition.
“The hands of Zerubbabel have laid the foundation — his hands shall also finish it.”
Not by his sufficiency — but by God’s word.
Completion is bound to promise.
O soul —
what God begins through thee,
He Himself undertakes to finish.
Chapter V

The Summons

Come, stand.
Not cleansed — but willing. Not worthy — but called.
Let the accusation speak. Let the garment be seen.
And remain — until the Lord answers.
O soul —
thy restoration is not negotiated.
It is declared.
Chapter VI

Garments Exchanged

G A R M E N T S   E X C H A N G E D
Before the throne the priest was found, His garments stained with sin; No plea arose, no righteous sound, No strength was left within.
The accuser stood at his right side, Each fault laid bare to view; Yet God Himself the charge denied, And spoke what none else knew.
“Remove the filth, and clothe him fair,” The word of mercy came; Not by his worth, nor by his care, But by the Lord’s own name.
O soul, stand still before thy God,
Let every covering fall;
For He who marks the guilty rod
Can cleanse and crown it all.
