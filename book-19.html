The Folio · Book 19

It Was Not Hidden From Thee

On the soul that begins to suspect that what it suffers is not incidental, but inherent to the way itself

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from Psalm 69 and the Gospel of Matthew — on the soul that has walked forward in sincerity, yet finds that what returns upon it is not ease, but cost; and must face whether this belongs to the path it has chosen.

Prologue

The Unequal Return

It began simply.
Nothing severe. Nothing marked.
A word was given — and came back heavier.
A small obedience — and something was taken.
Not enough to question. Not enough to conclude.
Yet — it did not feel equal. What was done lightly did not return lightly.
O soul — there are beginnings
where nothing appears wrong,
yet something does not return as it should.
Chapter I

The Repetition and the Pattern

It happened again.
Not the same — but alike.
Another step — another loss. Another trust — another strain.
Each could be explained.
Yet together — they did not rest.
O soul — what repeats without agreement
begins to press toward meaning.
It could no longer be set aside.
Too frequent to ignore. Too aligned to scatter.
What seemed separate began to gather.
O soul — there is a point
where experience refuses
to remain accidental.
It was not chosen. Not welcomed.
Yet it stood:
what was given to God returned with cost.
O soul — some patterns do not arise from error,
but from the path itself.
Chapter II

The Betrayal and the Exposure

Those who were near did not remain.
Not all at once — but enough.
Closeness did not preserve.
O soul — there is a following
that leads thee through
what Christ Himself was given to know.
What could have been hidden was not.
What might have remained whole was brought low.
Not by force — but by the way itself.
O soul — honour before God
may not shield thee from being brought down before men.
What had been spoken did not pass away.
It came back — not as memory, but as demand.
O soul — what thou hast said before God
will not remain as words;
it will return as requirement.
Chapter III

The Cross That Did Not Pass

It did not lift. It did not lessen.
It remained — as though placed.
Not an interruption — but something fixed.
O soul — there is a burden
that is not sent to pass through thee,
but to abide with thee.
It did not agree.
The flesh recoiled. The will resisted.
Yet the way did not bend.
O soul — what must die within thee
will not yield without struggle.
Chapter IV

The Cost of Remaining

To remain — was to give. To love — was to bear.
Nothing returned in kind.
O soul — there is a love
that does not meet itself again.
What had been done was not repaid. Not corrected. Not restored.
Only released.
O soul — forgiveness may close the past
without restoring what was lost within it.
Not all could be held. Not all could remain.
Some things fell away — not by desire, but by truth.
O soul — there is a separating
that is not escape, but obedience.
It was not scattered. It had not gone wrong.
It had followed — steadily, consistently.
What had come belonged to the way.

O soul — there is a knowing
that what thou hast suffered is not outside the path, but within it.
Chapter V

The Summons

Wilt thou remain if this is the way?
Wilt thou follow if it continues so?
Wilt thou accept that loss, testing, and silence
are not interruptions — but marks?

O soul — the cross is not encountered once; it is entered.
Chapter VI

It Was Not Hidden From Thee

I T   W A S   N O T   H I D D E N   F R O M   T H E E
A little given, more returned, It did not stand the same; What once seemed small came back with weight, And bore another name.
Again it came, though not alike, Yet pressing just the same; Till all that once seemed separate Returned with single aim.
The cross did not depart from me, But settled as my own; And all I gave before my God Was answered not, but grown.
O Lord, if this the path must be
For those who follow Thee;
Then keep me in it to the end,
And let me not turn free.
