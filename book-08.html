The Man of God from Judah

On obedience divided, and the cost of turning aside

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from 1 Kings 13 — on the man sent with a word he kept in part, and lost in the keeping of it.

Prologue

The Word Given

The word of the Lord came unto him.
It was not veiled. It was not broken.
“Go to Bethel. Cry against the altar. Eat no bread. Drink no water. Return not by the way thou camest.”
The charge stood whole before him. No shadow lay upon it. No second voice accompanied it.
O soul — when the Lord speaketh plainly,
the path is already set.
Chapter I

The Cry and the Refusal

He came to Bethel while the king stood by the altar. The fire burned. The priests moved. The people gathered near.
And he cried against the altar in the word of the Lord.
He named it. He declared its end.
“Behold, a child shall be born… and upon thee shall men’s bones be burnt.”
And the altar clave asunder. The ashes fell to the ground.
O soul — the word of the Lord
doth not tremble before kings.
The king stretched forth his hand. “Lay hold on him.”
But the hand withered where it was stretched. It could not be drawn back again.
O soul — when God resisteth,
strength faileth in the very act.
“Come home with me. Refresh thyself.”
But the man of God stood fast.
“If thou wilt give me half thine house, I will not go in with thee. I will eat no bread. I will drink no water in this place.”
And he turned to go another way.
O soul — obedience must remain
when the trial has passed.
Chapter II

The Other Voice

And there dwelt an old prophet in Bethel.
He found him sitting beneath an oak.
“Come home with me, and eat bread.”
But he answered as before. “I may not return with thee.”
Then the old prophet said, “I also am a prophet as thou art; and an angel spake unto me by the word of the Lord, saying, Bring him back.”
The word came near him, gentle upon the ear. It bore the likeness of truth.
Yet it was not of the Lord.
And he turned.
O soul — not every voice that speaketh in the name of the Lord
is sent of Him.
Chapter III

The Table and the Word Returns

He sat in the house of the one who had called him back.
Bread was set before him. Water was poured out.
The place he was sent to judge became the place he rested in.
The command lay broken at the table.
O soul — what is refused in strength
may return in ease.
And the word of the Lord came unto the prophet that brought him back.
“Thou hast disobeyed the mouth of the Lord. Thou hast not kept the commandment.”
The voice that had turned him aside now spoke the judgment upon him.
O soul — when the command is broken,
the word returneth as witness.
Chapter IV

The Lion in the Way

And he went his way.
The road lay as before. The dust rose as before.
Yet the word went with him no longer as guide, but as sentence.
O soul — the path may remain,
yet the standing is altered.
And a lion met him in the way. It slew him.
The ass stood by him. The lion stood by him.
The body was not torn. The beast departed not.
The judgment stood open before all that passed.
O soul — the works of God bear a mark
that cannot be mistaken.
Chapter V

The Divided Obedience

He had spoken the word of the Lord. He had stood before the king. He had refused the hand that offered him reward.
Yet he turned at the second voice.
The command was not kept whole. Not in part. But in its end.
O soul — the Lord requireth the whole obedience,
and not the beginning only.
Chapter VI

The Broken Path

T H E   B R O K E N   P A T H
The word was clear. The path was plain. No shadow veiled the way; Yet turning at another voice He cast the charge away.
He stood before the king unshaken, He would not take reward; Yet at the table of deceit He loosed the given word.
The lion stood. The body fell. The witness did remain; For God requireth all the path, And not the part made plain.
O soul, keep fast what thou hast heard,
Let no voice turn thee aside;
For life is found in holding still
Where once the Lord did guide.
