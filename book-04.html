That Which Was Not His

On stewardship, restraint, and increase under another’s hand

A prophetic series in five chapters — drawn from Genesis 30–31 — on Jacob under Laban; trained to steward the undeserving, leaving with God’s blessing, not his own.

Prologue

Arrival Empty

He came with nothing but a stone for pillow and a promise burning at Bethel. No flock answered his call, no silver weighed his belt. Only faith walked beside him into the house of the deceiver.
O soul, mark this well:
the Lord sends His servants forth empty
that He may fill them in secret.
Laban measured him with shifting eyes, changed wages as the wind changes. Jacob bent beneath the yoke, silent as the dust beneath his feet. Yet in that alien house the unseen Hand already stirred.
O soul, will you serve where no honour waits,
and still keep your heart alert?
Chapter I

The Yoke and the Increase

He led the flocks to pasture under skies that withheld their rain. Speckled, spotted, ringstraked — the increase rose like mist from dry ground. Not by rod of cunning, not by bargain sealed in twilight. Blessing flowed through hands that yielded all claim.
O soul, tremble:
obedience alone channels heaven’s flood.
Ten times the master altered the agreement, seeking to bind the blessing. Jacob adapted, patient as the slow turning of seasons. Still the ewes brought forth, still the rams grew strong. Increase answered faithfulness, never ownership.
O soul, release what you clutch;
the Lord multiplies what passes
through open hands.
Chapter II

Labour’s Hidden Vigil

By night he watched the folds, by day he walked the barren hills. Every lamb, every ewe, every shadowed corner fell under his care. The master prospered while Jacob’s own portion remained a promise deferred. Nothing was his to keep. Everything was his to tend.
O soul, steward what is lent thee,
though it bear another’s name.
Labour under the unworthy burned away all illusion of self. Patience grew deep roots; sight sharpened beyond the visible flock. The undeserving ate from tables Jacob’s obedience had spread. Even Laban marvelled:
“The Lord hath blessed me for thy sake.”

O soul, God trains His chosen
in the house of strangers.
Chapter III

Departure and What Remained

When the time came, Jacob rose to go. Flocks, wives, children, substance — he left behind the appearance of wealth. Yet the house he departed trembled at the absence. Laban pursued in wrath, for the blessing had been channelled, not possessed.
O soul, when you withdraw,
the unworthy discover
whose hand truly sustained them.
He departed with nothing new in his grasp, yet rich beyond measure. Faithful stewardship under unjust authority had become heaven’s hidden treasure. The skill no cunning could teach, the sight no scheming could purchase.
O soul, your refining may go unseen;
it prepares thee for greater folds.
Chapter IV

Modern Soul, Hearken

You labour now under ungrateful eyes, under hands that shift and scheme. Your increase feeds those who neither see nor thank. Do not despair. Do not grasp. Obedience still channels what craft cannot command.
The Lord watches.
The Lord multiplies selectively.
Your work is weighed in balances unseen. Diligence is noted; pride is exposed. Blessing flows only where faithfulness has cleared the way.
O soul, tremble:
God entrusts increase to those
who will not claim it.
Lift up your hands this day.
Yield fully to the refining yoke.

“Lord, let my labour be Thy channel, even to the unworthy.
Let my obedience unlock what no eye hath yet seen.”

Faithful stewardship under another’s hand
births increase beyond comprehension.
Chapter V

That Which Was Not His

T H A T   W H I C H   W A S   N O T   H I S  —  C O M M O N   M E T E R
Beneath the stranger’s watchful gaze I kept the speckled fold; No wage was sure, no hope of praise, Yet blessing uncontrolled.
Ten times the terms were bent like reeds, Yet still the increase came; Not mine the cunning, not my deeds, But God’s unquenchable flame.
I rose and left the gathered store, The house that knew my tread; Behind me closed the opened door — The void where blessing fled.
O soul, endure the thankless yoke, And tend what is not thine; The increase is the Lord’s to stoke, The labour — His design.
When thou depart, the masters mourn
The strength they never owned;
What passed through thee shall not return
Until thy God is known.
