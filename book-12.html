Be Thou to Us Instead of Eyes

On the soul that loses all guidance, and is given God instead

A prophetic series in six chapters — drawn from Exodus 18, 33, and Numbers 10 — on Moses, who sought a guide for the way, and was given the presence of God in place of it.

Prologue

What Was Not There

The camp moved. The people gathered. The burden remained.
Yet what had once been near was no longer with him.
His house was not beside him. His sons were not at his side.
The work stood. But something human was absent.
O soul — there are callings that continue,
while what is personal falls away.
Chapter I

The Burden and the Counsel

He stood before the people. Questions rose. Matters pressed. From morning until evening he bore them.
No equal stood with him. No voice shared the weight. The work was his — and remained his.
O soul — there is a labour that cannot be divided,
though many stand around thee.
Once, there had been counsel. A voice that ordered the burden. A presence that stood near.
It had come — and it had gone. The structure remained. The man did not.
O soul — God may permit help for a season,
and then remove it without replacing it.
Chapter II

The Appeal to Man

The way lay ahead. Not marked. Not certain.
A people to lead. A land not seen. The path required knowing — where to turn, where to rest.
The need was not strength. It was sight.
O soul — there are moments
when direction matters more than endurance.
So he spoke: “Leave us not.”
He turned to one who knew the ground. One who had walked those paths.
“Be to us instead of eyes.”
Not for all the way — but for the near journey. A little guidance. A little certainty.
O soul — it is no small thing
to ask another to see for thee.
The answer did not settle it. No firm joining. No sure remaining.
The help sought did not become possession. The path remained open — and unclear.
O soul — there are appeals
that do not return as strength.
Chapter III

The Need Turned Upward

So the need remained. The people still waited. The way still stretched forward.
Nothing had changed outwardly.
Only this had altered: He had asked — and was not secured.
O soul — there is a moment
when even the seeking of help
yields no ground.
Then the need shifted. Not outward now — but upward.
No longer: Who will guide us? But: What is with us?
The question changed its direction.
O soul — when no man can stand for thee,
the soul turns where it had not first looked.
Chapter IV

The Presence and the Rest

“My presence shall go with thee.”
It was not direction given. Not steps ordered.
No map unfolded. No path described.
Only this: Presence.
O soul — God may not show thee the way;
He may give Himself instead.
“I will give thee rest.”
Not from the journey. Not from the burden. But within it.
The way was not shortened. The people were not removed. Yet rest was spoken.
O soul — rest may be given
where nothing outward is resolved.
“If Thy presence go not with me…”
He did not ask for guidance now. He did not ask for signs.
Only this: That God Himself remain.
Better no movement — than movement without Him.
O soul — there is a place
where direction matters less
than companionship.
Chapter V

Beyond Guidance

“Show me Thy glory.”
Not the path. Not the outcome.
But God Himself.
The need had deepened. Sight of the way had become sight of Him.
O soul — when all lesser needs fall,
the soul asks not for help,
but for God.
The camp still moved. The desert remained. The people remained.
No map was given. No easier road appeared.
Yet something unseen had taken the place of what was missing.
He walked — not because he saw, but because God was with him.
O soul — there is a walking
that no longer depends on sight.
Chapter VI

Instead of Eyes

I N S T E A D   O F   E Y E S
I sought for one to guide my way, To mark the path ahead; A voice to stand in place of sight, Where I in doubt was led.
I asked for eyes to lead me on, Through ground I did not know; Yet none remained to take that place, Or teach me where to go.
Then God did not reveal the path, Nor set the journey plain; He gave not sight — but gave Himself, And all was changed within.
O soul, seek not for eyes alone,
Nor hands to lead thy stride;
For God may take what thou wouldst use —
And walk Himself beside.
